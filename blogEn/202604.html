Sleep and Astral Projection

I’ve been quite busy lately, which has made it difficult to find time for meditation. Astral projection requires meditation, and meditation requires sufficient sleep. If you are sleep-deprived, the pull of sleep becomes too strong, making it impossible to maintain the focus needed for meditation.

①Preparation: After taking a nap, I woke myself up completely. I then entered a reclining position and employed the same technique as last time: a meditation focused on maintaining a continuous flow of energy at the crown of the head.

②Partial Separation: After a while, parts of my body, such as my legs, began to float. My astral body would repeatedly rise up and then slip back into the physical body. It is counterproductive to panic during this stage. It is best to stay relaxed, go with the flow, and avoid trying to move the astral body through sheer force of will.

③Stabilization: Eventually, I felt certain that my astral body had fully exited the physical body and stabilized. I was able to move through the room at will. Although the rooms were dark, I explored all three and confirmed that there were no significant differences from the physical world.

④The Mantra and Return: While in the astral state, I attempted the Vajra Chanting . Shortly after finishing, I returned to my physical body.
