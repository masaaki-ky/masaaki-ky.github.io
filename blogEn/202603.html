Astral Projection Technique and Recent Success

Lately, my success rate with astral projection has significantly improved using the following method. I typically practice this when I am alert, such as after a nap.

The Induction Process:
①While lying down, I bend my knees and lift my hips. I tense my back and abdominal muscles, applying concentrated pressure to the Dantian.
②This generates energy within the Dantian, which then rises through the central channel until I feel it reach the crown of my head.
③Once the energy reaches the crown, I lower my legs and remain lying flat, maintaining total focus on the Dantian.
④While feeling the energy at the crown, I maintain a state of no-mind (thoughtlessness).
⑤This leads naturally into a "waking dream," which then transitions into full astral projection.

Stabilization via Guhyagarbha Tantra:
During this astral projection, I attempted to stabilize my astral form using the Vajra Recitation from the Guhyagarbha Tantra:

① I visualized the syllables Oṃ, Āḥ, and Hūṃ at the specific energy centers of my astral body.
② I synchronized the mental recitation of each mantra with the cycle of inhaling, retaining, and exhaling energy.

In this particular experience, the astral projection ended while I was still floating in my room, just as I had finished visualizing the syllables.
